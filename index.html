Memorandum

TO: Commanding Officers, Detachment 88 Anti-Terror Unit (Densus 88); Mobile Brigade Corps (Brimob) Regional Commanders
FROM: DS-ATA Program Office, U.S. Embassy Jakarta
DATE: June 2026
SUBJECT: Counterterrorism Tactical Operations Training — Course Notification and Participant Preparation Requirements

Purpose

This memorandum provides official notification of an upcoming Counterterrorism Tactical Operations Training (CTOT) course to be conducted under the auspices of the U.S. Department of State Diplomatic Security Antiterrorism Assistance (DS-ATA) Program. The course is offered in coordination with the Indonesian National Police (Polri) in support of bilateral law enforcement cooperation objectives.

Scope

This training is applicable to sworn law enforcement personnel holding the rank of Inspector (Inspektur) or above, currently assigned to counterterrorism, tactical response, or special operations units. Personnel must be physically active on duty and hold valid security clearances at the appropriate level. The training will be conducted in Jakarta and shall not exceed fifteen (15) working days in duration.

Training Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, participants will demonstrate proficiency in: (1) threat assessment and target analysis in complex, high-density environments; (2) tactical entry, room clearance, and hostage rescue procedures in accordance with internationally recognized standards; (3) command-and-control communications protocols during multi-agency joint operations; (4) use-of-force doctrine consistent with applicable Indonesian law and international human rights standards; and (5) post-incident documentation and evidence preservation procedures.

Course Components

The curriculum comprises classroom instruction (40%), practical exercises (45%), and evaluations (15%). Subject matter experts from the U.S. Department of State and contracted law enforcement training specialists will deliver instruction in English, with consecutive interpretation provided throughout all sessions. Curriculum materials, including technical reference guides and case studies, will be distributed in bilingual format.

Participant Responsibilities

Nominating units are responsible for ensuring that all designated participants: submit completed pre-course documentation packages no later than fourteen (14) calendar days prior to the course commencement date; undergo and pass a mandatory pre-participation medical examination; maintain a minimum 90% attendance rate across all scheduled sessions; and adhere to all conduct, uniform, and equipment requirements as specified in Annex A of this memorandum.

Safety Requirements

All live-fire exercises will be conducted in strict accordance with DS-ATA Range Safety Protocols. Participants must successfully complete a weapon familiarization and safety qualification assessment prior to engagement in any live-fire activity. The U.S. Embassy retains the authority to remove any participant from live-fire exercises based on demonstrated safety deficiencies, without prejudice to continued participation in non-live-fire components of the course.

Certification

Participants who satisfy all attendance, practical, and written assessment requirements will receive a Certificate of Course Completion jointly issued by the U.S. Embassy Jakarta DS-ATA Program and the Indonesian National Police Training Division (Lemdiklat Polri). Certificates will be dispatched to nominating units within thirty (30) business days of course conclusion.

Kedutaan Besar Amerika Serikat berwenang untuk memindahkan peserta dari latihan tembak langsung berdasarkan kekurangan keselamatan yang teridentifikasi, tanpa mengurangi hak peserta untuk melanjutkan komponen kursus yang tidak melibatkan tembak langsung.

Sertifikasi

Sertifikat akan dikirimkan kepada satuan penominasi dalam waktu tiga puluh (30) hari kerja setelah kursus berakhir.

Meeting Background

A tri-party coordination meeting was convened on June 10, 2026, at the U.S. Embassy Jakarta Regional Security Office conference room. The purpose of the meeting was to finalize operational and logistical arrangements for the upcoming Advanced Crisis Negotiation Course (ACNC) scheduled for August 2026, and to address unresolved coordination items from the previous quarter's training cycle.

Participants

Regional Security Officer, U.S. Embassy Jakarta (Chair); DS-ATA Program Coordinator, U.S. Embassy Jakarta; DS-ATA Training Specialist (TDY), Washington D.C.; Deputy Director for Cooperation, Polri Lemdiklat; Head of Training Operations, Polri Detachment 88; Polri Liaison Officer to the Embassy; Consecutive Interpreter (present throughout all sessions).

Discussion Topics

The meeting addressed four primary agenda items. First, the parties reviewed participant nomination procedures, noting delays in the submission of pre-course medical clearance documentation by nominating units in three regional commands. DS-ATA and Polri agreed to implement a unified online submission portal for all pre-course documentation effective the following training cycle. Second, the parties discussed the integration of newly developed scenario-based modules into the ACNC curriculum, including urban crisis simulation, media relations during active incidents, and inter-agency communication protocols. Third, venue and logistics arrangements for the August course were confirmed, including billeting, ground transportation, and catering requirements for forty (40) participants and ten (10) instructors. Fourth, the parties reviewed feedback from the preceding Tactical Communications Training course, identifying three areas for curriculum refinement: pacing of technical instruction sessions, adequacy of simultaneous interpretation logistics, and diversity of scenario case studies to reflect Indonesian regional contexts.

Operational Concerns

The Polri Detachment 88 representative raised a concern regarding the overlap of the proposed ACNC schedule with a pre-existing Polri internal training commitment affecting two of the nominated participants. DS-ATA confirmed that, subject to the RSO's approval, the course schedule could accommodate a one-day delayed start for the affected participants without compromising overall course integrity. A formal schedule adjustment request is to be submitted by Polri Lemdiklat within five (5) business days.

Resource Requirements

The following resources were confirmed as required for the August ACNC: forty (40) participant workbooks in bilingual format; ten (10) instructor guide copies; two (2) dedicated consecutive interpreters for the full duration of the course; audio-visual equipment including two projectors and one wireless microphone system; and access to a scenario simulation facility within a thirty-minute driving radius of the Embassy.

Course Schedule

The Advanced Crisis Negotiation Course is scheduled for August 11–22, 2026 (ten working days). Day 1: Orientation, administrative briefings, safety briefing. Days 2–4: Foundational theory — negotiation frameworks, behavioral assessment, stakeholder communication. Days 5–7: Scenario-based practical exercises (Phase I — low-complexity scenarios). Days 8–9: Scenario-based practical exercises (Phase II — high-complexity, multi-party scenarios). Day 10: Written assessment, course evaluations, certificate ceremony.

Action Items

Polri Lemdiklat: Submit formal schedule adjustment request by June 17. All nominating units: Complete pre-course documentation submission via new portal by July 28. DS-ATA Coordinator: Confirm interpreter procurement and AV equipment delivery by July 15. RSO: Approve adjusted schedule and issue formal course authorization by July 1.

Follow-Up Plans

A follow-up coordination call is scheduled for July 5, 2026, to confirm the status of all action items prior to the documentation submission deadline. Meeting minutes will be distributed to all participants within three (3) business days of this meeting. The DS-ATA Program Coordinator will serve as the primary point of contact for any intervening issues.

Ransomware Attacks on Critical Infrastructure: Southeast Asia in the Crosshairs

Security Monitoring Report — For Official Awareness Distribution

Cybersecurity analysts across Southeast Asia have raised alarms over a significant uptick in ransomware attacks targeting critical infrastructure sectors, including public utilities, transportation networks, and government data systems. In the past eighteen months, documented incidents in the region have increased by an estimated 42 percent, with threat actors increasingly prioritizing entities whose operational continuity is essential to national security and public safety.

Indonesian institutions have not been immune to this trend. A number of government agencies and state-owned enterprise networks reported suspicious activity consistent with initial-stage ransomware deployment in the first quarter of 2026, though full-scale encryption attacks were mitigated in most documented cases through rapid incident response protocols. Cybersecurity agencies have noted that threat actors are leveraging publicly known vulnerabilities in widely deployed enterprise software, combined with social engineering techniques targeting government employees with administrative access to critical systems.

Law enforcement agencies, including Polri's Cyber Crime Unit (Dittipidsiber), have intensified coordination with international partners including INTERPOL's Cybercrime Directorate and the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation to attribute attacks and disrupt threat actor infrastructure. Three suspects connected to a regional ransomware syndicate were apprehended in early 2026 following a joint operation conducted under the ASEAN Cybercrime Operations Desk framework.

Cybersecurity experts emphasize that the primary vulnerability vector for government institutions remains human error: specifically, employees clicking on phishing links, using unsecured personal devices on institutional networks, or failing to apply software patches in a timely manner. Security awareness training programs targeting government personnel at all levels are identified as the most cost-effective mitigation measure against ransomware risk.

DS-ATA and the Embassy Regional Security Office recommend the following immediate measures for all Embassy-affiliated personnel and Indonesian government partners: enable multi-factor authentication on all institutional accounts; report any suspicious email activity to the Embassy IT Security Officer within one hour of detection; do not connect personal devices to Embassy or Embassy-affiliated networks; and participate in the upcoming Embassy-hosted Security Awareness Briefing scheduled for July 2026.

Serangan Ransomware terhadap Infrastruktur Kritis: Asia Tenggara dalam Bidikan

Laporan Pemantauan Keamanan — Untuk Distribusi Kesadaran Resmi

Para analis keamanan siber di seluruh Asia Tenggara telah membunyikan alarm atas lonjakan signifikan serangan ransomware yang menyasar sektor infrastruktur kritis, termasuk utilitas publik, jaringan transportasi, dan sistem data pemerintahan. Dalam delapan belas bulan terakhir, insiden yang terdokumentasi di kawasan ini meningkat sekitar 42 persen, dengan pelaku ancaman yang semakin memprioritaskan entitas yang keberlangsungan operasionalnya sangat penting bagi keamanan nasional dan keselamatan publik.

Institusi-institusi Indonesia tidak luput dari tren ini. Sejumlah lembaga pemerintah dan jaringan perusahaan BUMN melaporkan aktivitas mencurigakan yang konsisten dengan tahap awal penerapan ransomware pada kuartal pertama 2026, meskipun serangan enkripsi skala penuh berhasil diantisipasi di sebagian besar kasus yang terdokumentasi melalui protokol respons insiden yang cepat. Badan-badan keamanan siber mencatat bahwa pelaku ancaman memanfaatkan kerentanan yang sudah diketahui publik dalam perangkat lunak korporat yang banyak digunakan, dikombinasikan dengan teknik rekayasa sosial yang menyasar pegawai pemerintah dengan akses administratif ke sistem-sistem kritis.

Instansi penegak hukum, termasuk Direktorat Tindak Pidana Siber Polri (Dittipidsiber), telah mengintensifkan koordinasi dengan mitra internasional termasuk Direktorat Kejahatan Siber INTERPOL dan Biro Investigasi Federal Amerika Serikat untuk mengidentifikasi pelaku dan melumpuhkan infrastruktur ancaman. Tiga tersangka yang terkait dengan sindikat ransomware regional ditangkap pada awal 2026 menyusul operasi gabungan yang dilaksanakan dalam kerangka ASEAN Cybercrime Operations Desk.

Para pakar keamanan siber menekankan bahwa vektor kerentanan utama bagi institusi pemerintah tetap berupa kesalahan manusia: secara spesifik, pegawai yang mengklik tautan phishing, menggunakan perangkat pribadi yang tidak aman pada jaringan institusional, atau gagal menerapkan pembaruan perangkat lunak tepat waktu. Program pelatihan kesadaran keamanan yang menyasar personel pemerintah di semua tingkatan diidentifikasi sebagai langkah mitigasi paling efektif dari segi biaya terhadap risiko ransomware.

DS-ATA dan Kantor Keamanan Regional Kedutaan merekomendasikan langkah-langkah segera berikut bagi seluruh personel yang berafiliasi dengan Kedutaan dan mitra pemerintah Indonesia: aktifkan autentikasi multi-faktor pada semua akun institusional; laporkan aktivitas email mencurigakan kepada Petugas Keamanan TI Kedutaan dalam waktu satu jam setelah terdeteksi; jangan menghubungkan perangkat pribadi ke jaringan Kedutaan atau jaringan yang terafiliasi dengan Kedutaan; dan ikuti Pengarahan Kesadaran Keamanan yang diselenggarakan Kedutaan yang dijadwalkan pada Juli 2026.

Translator Notes
Loanwords in cybersecurity: Terms like "ransomware," "phishing," and "malware" are retained as loanwords rather than translated. Indonesian cybersecurity authorities (BSSN) use these terms in official communications, and attempting to translate them would create unfamiliar neologisms that could confuse specialist readers.
"Attribution": The cybersecurity term "to attribute attacks" (to link attacks to specific actors) was rendered as "mengidentifikasi pelaku" (to identify perpetrators) rather than the direct loan "atribusi," which is understood within technical circles but less accessible in a document intended for broader government distribution.

Official Course Announcement

Course: Advanced Investigative Interview Techniques (AIIT)
Hosted by: DS-ATA Program, U.S. Embassy Jakarta, in coordination with Polri Criminal Investigation Division (Bareskrim)
Date: September 15–19, 2026
Venue: Bareskrim Training Center, Jakarta Selatan

Eligibility Requirements

This course is open to active Polri investigators holding the rank of Second Lieutenant (Ipda) through Lieutenant Colonel (AKBP), currently assigned to criminal investigation units at the provincial or national level. Nominees must have a minimum of two (2) years of investigative experience and must not have attended an AIIT-equivalent course within the preceding three years. Nominees with English proficiency at a conversational level or above are strongly encouraged; however, consecutive interpretation will be provided throughout the course.

Schedule

Day 1 (Monday, September 15): Administrative registration, course orientation, and introduction to interview frameworks. Day 2 (Tuesday, September 16): Cognitive interview techniques and memory enhancement protocols. Day 3 (Wednesday, September 17): Behavioral analysis, deception detection, and statement analysis. Day 4 (Thursday, September 18): Practical interview simulations with role players; peer and instructor feedback sessions. Day 5 (Friday, September 19): Written assessment, course review, certificate presentation ceremony (16:00 WIB).

Venue and Logistics

The course will be held at the Bareskrim Training Center, Jl. Trunojoyo No. 3, Jakarta Selatan. Participants from outside the Greater Jakarta area are eligible to apply for subsidized billeting at the Bareskrim Guest Facilities. Requests for billeting must be submitted with the pre-course documentation package. Ground transportation from the Bareskrim complex to the course venue will be arranged for all registered participants.

Dress Code

Participants are required to wear the Class B service uniform (PDL) for all practical exercise sessions. Business attire or Class A service uniform is required for the certificate presentation ceremony. Civilian participants, if any, are required to wear professional business attire throughout the course duration.

Required Documents

Participants must submit the following to their nominating unit's administrative office no later than August 29, 2026: completed DS-ATA Course Registration Form (Form DS-ATA/REG-2026); valid institutional ID and copy of most recent rank certificate; pre-course medical clearance from an authorized medical officer; and written approval from immediate supervisor. Incomplete submissions will not be processed.

Attendance Policy

Attendance at all scheduled sessions is mandatory. Participants who accumulate absences exceeding 10% of total course hours will be administratively withdrawn and will not be eligible for certification. Exceptions for medical emergencies must be documented and approved by the DS-ATA Program Coordinator no later than 24 hours after the missed session.

Safety Reminders

Personal firearms are not permitted inside the training facility. All participants are required to secure their service weapons at the Bareskrim armory upon arrival. The use of recording devices during instruction sessions is prohibited without prior written authorization from the DS-ATA Program Coordinator. Participants are reminded to adhere to all Embassy and Bareskrim facility security protocols at all times.

Interpretation Notes — Key Challenges
Rank rendering (Exchange 6): "Iptu" (Inspektur Polisi Satu) has no standard English translation. "First Inspector" was used as a functional equivalent consistent with how DS-ATA training documents have previously described Polri ranks to American personnel. A parenthetical gloss would be added in any written transcript.
Face-saving register (Exchange 38): The General's use of "bantuan satu arah" (one-directional assistance) is a diplomatically indirect way of pushing back against a donor-recipient framing. The interpreter preserved the diplomatic indirectness of the original, avoiding over-literalism that might have sharpened the critique beyond what was intended.
"Instruktur asing" → "international instructors" (Exchange 24): The Indonesian "asing" (foreign/alien) carries a stronger othering connotation than "international." In a diplomatic setting between institutional equals, "international instructors" is the contextually appropriate rendering.
Consecutive vs. simultaneous (general): In consecutive interpretation, complete units of meaning — not individual sentences — are the unit of interpretation. Several exchanges above involve multi-sentence utterances that were held in memory and rendered as coherent wholes, using note-taking conventions for key figures, names, and action items.
